Commitment

People are less likely to commit crimes when they know that they have something to lose.
If a person has invested a lot of time and energy into achieving certain accomplishments and goals, then they have a lot to lose if they commit a crime.
Commitment could be referenced as an attachment to something personal rather than something external.

If someone has good grades, then they are less likely to be late, commit crimes, and they are more likely to pay attention in class.

This doesn't work in every case. We often see people in power commiting tax fraud, draft evasion, or other sexual related crimes.
